The Economic Way of Thinking

Overview

The Economic Way of Thinking goes beyond standard graphs and equations to teach the logic of economic reasoning. Paul Heyne, along with Peter Boettke and David Prychitko, presents economics not as a set of facts to be memorized, but as a disciplined method of thinking about the world. This 13th Edition focuses primarily on microeconomics, exploring how individual choices under scarcity lead to complex market order. It covers essential topics like demand, supply, and marginal analysis using clear, non-technical language, making it ideal for understanding the "unintended consequences" of human action.
